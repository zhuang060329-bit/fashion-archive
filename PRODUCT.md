# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Design-literate viewers, fashion industry observers, potential employers reviewing a portfolio. They arrive desktop-first, expect editorial intelligence over decoration, and read critically. Context: deliberate browsing, not discovery. They have seen generic fashion sites; this must feel categorically different.

## Product Purpose

An independent educational and editorial portfolio project: a single-page long-scroll fashion history archive covering post-1970 trends, presented as an intelligence dossier. Not a blog, not e-commerce, not a template. Success: the viewer reads longer than intended and leaves with a concrete mental model of each era.

## Brand Personality

Archival. Precise. Unsentimental.

Voice: a fashion intelligence analyst writing for industry insiders, not a lifestyle blogger writing for consumers. Reference tone: V&A Museum exhibit copy meets Cathy Horyn review meets classified-file index.

## Anti-references

- Generic fashion editorial sites (Vogue.com, Hypebeast — too commercial, too grid-heavy)
- SaaS-style "premium" dark UIs (rounded cards, glow effects, glassmorphism)
- Instagram-aesthetic fashion portfolios (editorial photography as hero, decorative typography)
- Template sites with four-card sections and "seamless experience" copy
- Particle backgrounds, full-page gradients, any post-2020 motion-first web trends

## Design Principles

1. **Document, don't decorate.** Every visual element (grain, scanlines, corner marks, era numbers) must function as archival metadata, not decoration.
2. **Typography carries the argument.** Cormorant Garamond is the editorial voice; IBM Plex Mono is the classification system. Neither is decorative.
3. **Motion reveals, it does not perform.** Scroll animations surface content in the order a reader would encounter it. Animation that exists only to show off is removed.
4. **Restraint is the aesthetic.** High visual complexity is achieved through density and precision, not through effects. A well-set page of type is the target.
5. **Factual grounding is non-negotiable.** Confidence levels (verified / widely-reported / contested) are surfaced at the source level, not hidden.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum for color contrast
- Full prefers-reduced-motion support: all animations have instant/static alternatives
- Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements (case fragments)
- No motion that cannot be skipped
